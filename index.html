Frequently Asked

The questions we hear before every engagement.

What makes this different from hiring a RevOps consultant?
Most RevOps consultants audit, recommend, and leave. We build. Every engagement ends with live infrastructure running in your stack, not a 40-slide deck. We also only work with a small number of clients at once, so you're talking to the person who built your system, not a project coordinator.
What stage of company is this right for?
We work best with growth-stage companies, typically Series A through Series C, that have product-market fit but haven't systematized how they sell. You probably have reps, maybe a RevOps hire, and a CRM that isn't working the way it should. If you're pre-product, we're probably not the right fit yet.
How long does a typical engagement run?
Most core engagements run 10 to 14 weeks, depending on scope. Audit takes two weeks. Architecture and build happen in parallel sprints over the following 8 to 10 weeks. Transfer and activation is the final two weeks. We don't do open-ended retainers. Every engagement has a defined end state.
Do you replace our existing team or tools?
Neither. We work inside your existing stack and alongside your existing team. We're not there to prove your tools are wrong; we're there to make them work together. Rip-and-replace is almost never the answer and almost always sold by vendors, not operators.
What's the investment range?
Engagements are priced based on scope and company size. We don't publish rates because every situation is different. What we can tell you is that our pricing is fixed-fee, there are no ongoing fees after delivery, and the people you talk to are the people doing the work. We discuss this on the first call.
Why a waitlist? Can't I just reach out?
We intentionally limit the number of active engagements to four at a time. The waitlist is how we manage that. When a slot opens, we review recent applications and reach out to the highest-fit candidates. Joining the list is the fastest way to get on our radar for the next available slot.
